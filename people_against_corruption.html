THE TEAM

AJ Vicente

AJ Vicente

“I grew up in central New Jersey where the mood was pessimistic and dismissive for making life better for one another. As I entered my adult years, I started to fall into this realm of personal apathy until I felt the need to improve not just my life, but everyone else’s around me. Every time I started looking for a path to start making change, I was abruptly interrupted by the massive amounts of corruption in our political system. I realized, if I wanted to make a change to improve the general well-being of others, corruption needs to be abolished today.”

Jacqi Inouye

Jacqi Inouye

“As I drive down the streets of Los Angeles, where I was born and raised, I can visibly see what corruption can lead to: Mass homelessness, starving children, and a devastatingly under-educated public. I learned that in order to start the healing process, we need to weed out the root problem of our political system, institutionalized corporate person-hood. We need a government that is incentivized to solve people’s problems and not just do the bidding of the rich. This is worth fighting for and I’m going to do just that.”

Kay Saeyang

Kay Saeyang

“I am a wife and mother of two awesome girls. As a mother, I am very frustrated because I want to leave a better world for our children but our government hinders us from doing so. They are so corrupt and don’t represent the people. One of my favorite quotes is from Maya Angelou, “When people show you who they are, believe them.” The politicians have shown us over and over and over who they are. They are corrupted and greedy. We need to believe them. We need to fix our government.”
